Lossless vs Lossy Audio
Like a guest from early 2000’s, this game uses uncompressed WAV files for audio, and they take 6.4 GB of disk space. If you think that it’s an overkill, you can download and install lossy audio files. I encoded them with state-of-the-art Opus audio codec, with variable bitrate from 64 Kbps (mono) to 128 Kbps (stereo). The same encoder I used in my Black Ops 3 repack and ~90% of users couldn’t distinguish original audio from Opus-encoded. But of course, decision is yours.
